Meet HariChandana

Administrator, Eco Warrior, Changemaker

HariChandana's journey combines global exposure with local impact. With degrees from the University of Hyderabad and LSE, she transitioned from a high-paying London job to IAS in 2010...

She has held key positions such as Assistant Collector in Vishakapatnam, Sub Collector in Vijayawada, Zonal Commissioner at GHMC, Collector of Narayanpet and Nalgonda, Food Safety Commissioner, Director of AYUSH, Director of Municipal Administration, Special Secretary in the Roads & Buildings Department, Telangana, and Director General of the National Academy of Construction (NAC). She currently serves as District Collector Hyderabad, appointed in June 2025 during a major Telangana bureaucratic reshuffle.

Her governance style is marked by a commitment to sustainability, innovation, and grassroots involvement. From smart infrastructure to women's economic empowerment and climate resilience, HariChandana leads with empathy, efficiency, and bold vision.

My Journey So Far

My education and experiences from every phase of my life have molded me into the person I am today.

🎓
Schooling & Graduation

Completed schooling at St. Ann’s, Hyderabad, and earned a bachelor's degree in Economics, Political Science & Public Administration from St. Ann’s College, Hyderabad

🎓
Master’s Degrees

MA in Political Science from University of Hyderabad

MSc in Environmental Economics from the London School of Economics (LSE)

🎓
Early Career

Worked at World Bank and BP Shell in London before leaving for civil service

💼
UPSC & Cadre

Cracked the UPSC in 2009 (second attempt) and joined the IAS in 2010, choosing her home cadre, Telangana

💼
GHMC Roles

Served as Zonal Commissioner (West Zone) and Additional Commissioner with GHMC—led urban infrastructure and waste management reforms

💼
District Collector Positions:

Narayanpet

As DM, directed COVID‑19 response (testing, drones, ration delivery, telemedicine) and engaged 4,000+ women in mask production

Nalgonda

Oversaw district development and skill-building/job initiatives

Hyderabad

Appointed Collector in June 2025 during major Telangana bureaucratic reshuffle

State Secretary Roles

Held positions such as Special Secretary, Roads & Buildings Department; Director of AYUSH; Director of Municipal Administration; State Nodal Officer for Praja Vani

Environment & Waste

Championed Hyderabad's green efforts—plastic recycling into tiles, upcycled furniture in parks, dog parks from dump yards, landfill-to‑park conversions

Introduced “Pet Parks,” “She Toilets,” “She Marts,” mobile women's sanitation, and community food-sharing units (“Feed the Need,” “Give and Share”)
